01 — Brand Audit
What exists, honestly assessed.

The brand has genuine soul — handcrafted garments, South African made, with clear design intent. The assets reviewed show a founder with real aesthetic sensibility. But the digital presence doesn't yet match the quality of what she actually makes.

Strengths
Logo has editorial elegance — SF monogram with botanical accent works well
Swing tags + woven labels show genuine brand investment
"Made in South Africa" is a powerful, underused asset
Muslin & natural fabrics position the brand toward conscious luxury
Instagram shows real range — styling, behind-the-scenes, Cape Town aesthetic
Weaknesses
Summer 2026 promo uses chaotic paint-splash — wrong brand signal
Handwritten annotations on product sheets undermine premium positioning
Instagram bio reads as a small business, not a design label
No consistent photographic style across the feed
Collection naming feels ad hoc — no coherent seasonal identity
Opportunities
Cape Town's creative scene is globally coveted — lean into it
African-made luxury is a premium category with global appetite
Muslin + breathable fabrics speak directly to sustainability trend
Founder-as-designer story is compelling and under-told
Modest fashion is a fast-growing international market
Inconsistencies to Fix
Yellow sunflower stickers feel casual vs. the editorial swing tags
Bold type on promo content vs. refined serif on collection covers
"Get ready to be amazed!" CTA tone vs. understated garment quality
Product photography lighting and styling varies significantly

04 — Customer Personas
She exists. Know her.
Amara
The Conscious Professional · 32–42
Works in design, education, or creative industries. Knows what she wants and dresses accordingly. Shops less but buys better. Values knowing who made her clothes and where. Follows Zara, Poetry, and independent Cape Town labels. Influences her community without trying to.
Nadia
The Modern Modest Woman · 25–38
Values comfort, coverage, and elegance simultaneously. Frustrated by fashion that either ignores her or tokenises her. Finds Sunflower's silhouettes — flowing, covered, intentional — genuinely freeing. Shops online, shares recommendations in her community circles.
Leila
The Gift-Giver · 35–55
Buys for birthdays, baby showers, graduations. Wants to give something meaningful — not a voucher, not a mass-market piece. When she discovers Sunflower, it becomes her go-to for women she respects. Loves the packaging. Photographs the gift.
Zara
The Diaspora Customer · 28–45
South African living in London, Amsterdam, or Melbourne. Homesick in a quiet way. Buying a Sunflower piece is buying Cape Town air. Pays international shipping without hesitation. Sends pieces to family back home. Her Instagram post is worth five ads.

About / Brand Story
Farah designs from instinct and from experience.
She started Sunflower Designs because she couldn't find clothes that felt right — not too formal, not too casual, always somehow not quite hers. So she made them herself. From a table in Cape Town, she cuts, drapes, stitches, and considers. The result is a small label with a clear point of view: clothing that a woman chooses, rather than settles for.
Collection — Summer 2026

09 — Mobile Experience
The phone is the primary store.

The majority of Sunflower's customers discover and buy via Instagram → mobile browser. Every design decision must work thumb-first. Key principles:

Navigation
Sticky minimal header. Brand name only. Three-line menu. Cart icon. Nothing else.
Product Images
Full-width swipeable gallery. Front, back, detail, on-body. No thumbnails.
Add to Cart
Persistent bottom bar. Size selector inline. One tap to bag. No modal interruptions.
Checkout
PayFast + credit card. WhatsApp order confirmation. Single-page flow. No account required.
10 — Implementation Notes
How to build this.
Recommended Stack
Shopify (Prestige or Dawn theme, heavily customised) or Next.js + Medusa.js for full control. PayFast for SA payments. Cloudflare for performance.
Photography Brief
Natural light only. Linen, concrete, and Cape fynbos as surfaces. No white seamless studio. Model must feel relatable, not aspirational-distant.
Instagram Strategy
Retire the paint-splash aesthetic. Establish a 3-grid visual system: product editorial, behind-the-scenes making, and Cape Town location content.
Launch Sequence
Teaser: "Something is coming." Brand film: 60-second founder story. Drop: Summer 2026 with waitlist access first. WhatsApp broadcast to existing customers.
